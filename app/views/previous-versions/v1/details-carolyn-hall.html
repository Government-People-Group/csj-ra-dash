About the role

This is a permanent role, working 37.5 hours per week.

You will be part of the Emergency Education Strategy team, supporting the Lead Strategy Advisors. Their focus is planning education strategies which might be implemented in times of national emergency.

Your line manager will be the Lead Strategy Advisor you are assigned to.


You will have key responsibilities to carry out on a day-to-day basis, like:

  • provide administrative support to the team
  • ensure projects focus on outcomes and deliver on time
  • assisting in the development of proposals for funding including drafting up content, editing and checking accuracy
  • drafting and providing content for progress reports on projects including extracting and collating information
  • assisting the team in delivering key milestones and evidencing action logs
  • setting up and supporting key stakeholder meetings and track progress on agreed actions

Location

You will be based in one of our Education Centres. When you apply you can state which Education Centre you would prefer to be based at, although we cannot guarantee you will be allocated to the Centre you prefer.

The Education Centres you can choose from are located in:

  • East Midlands (England), East of England, London (region), North East England, North West England, Scotland, South East England
We may be able to offer hybrid working for this role, where you can work partly from an Education Centre and partly from home. You can discuss this with us if you are offered a role.

What we're looking for


Essential criteria

For this role, you must:

  • pass, or have already passed, a Baseline Personnel Security Standard (BPSS) check
    see more about BPSS
    The BPSS checks are designed to verify your:
    • Identity
    • Nationality and Immigration Status (including an entitlement to undertake the work in question) - see the next section on Nationality requirements for more information
    • Employment history (past 3 years)
    • Criminal record (unspent convictions only)

    Additionally, prospective employees are required to give a reasonable account of any significant periods (6 months or more in the past 3 years) of time spent abroad.

  • meet the Nationality requirements for this role
    see more about nationality requirements
    This job is broadly open to the following groups:
    • UK nationals
    • nationals of the Republic of Ireland
    • nationals of Commonwealth countries who have the right to work in the UK
    • nationals of the EU, Switzerland, Norway, Iceland or Liechtenstein and family members of those nationalities with settled or pre-settled status under the European Union Settlement Scheme (EUSS) (opens in a new window)
    • nationals of the EU, Switzerland, Norway, Iceland or Liechtenstein and family members of those nationalities who have made a valid application for settled or pre-settled status under the European Union Settlement Scheme (EUSS)
    • individuals with limited leave to remain or indefinite leave to remain who were eligible to apply for EUSS on or before 31 December 2020
    • Turkish nationals, and certain family members of Turkish nationals, who have accrued the right to work in the Civil Service

Desirable criteria

For this role, you should be able to:

  • deal with wide ranging enquiries and follow through to resolution
  • work on your own initiative as well as collaboratively as part of a team
  • quickly develop working relationships
  • develop solutions to address problems
  • work flexibly to meet ever changing priorities
  • manage a diverse workload
  • utilise excellent communications skills, both oral and written


We welcome applications from the following schemes:

  • Great Place to work for Veterans
  • Prison leaver recruitment

We also welcome applications from anyone who may want reasonable adjustments or support, whether during the application process itself or if they are offered the job. There is more information at the bottom of this page.

What we offer

Benefits include:

  • Learning and development tailored to your role
  • An environment with flexible working options
  • A culture encouraging inclusion and diversity
  • A Civil Service pension with an average employer contribution of 27%
  • Entitlement to a minimum start of 23 days annual leave

The application process

  1. The CV and Personal Statement you submit as part of your application will be assessed against the following Success Profile behaviours:
    1. Managing a Quality Service
    2. Delivering at Pace
    3. Working Together
    4. Communicating and Influencing
  2. if your application passes this assessment, you will be invited to an interview. The format of the interview will be given to you in advance so you will know how to prepare for it
  3. once all interviews have been completed, you will be notified of the outcome by email and through your Civil Service Jobs account
  4. if successful you will receive a provisional job offer from us, including your starting salary, place of work and your working pattern if applicable
  5. If you accept this provisional offer, pre-employment checks will commence and once they are cleared, the vacancy holder will contact you with details of a start date

Important

Adjustments and support for your application

We want to make sure that our application process is fair to everyone. If you feel there are reasonable adjustments to the application process that we could make or support we could give you, you will be able to tell us once you start the application process.

If you have any questions about the adjustments or support we could make for you, please contact the Emergency Education Strategy team below.

If you have any questions about this role, you can contact the Emergency Education Strategy team on (0191) 2862862 or emergency.educ.strategy@dofe.gov.uk
